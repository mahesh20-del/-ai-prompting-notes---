Phase 1: Foundations (The "Identity & Boundary" Phase)  
In this phase, we stop treating the AI as a search engine and start treating it as a Specialized Asset. The objective here is to master Role Prompting and Context & Constraints. These are the pillars that determine the quality of every output you receive from an AI.
 
**1. Role Prompting/Advanced Role Prompting (The "Expert" Persona/The "Persona" Vector):**  
Instead of asking a general question, we assign the AI a specific identity. This triggers the model to access specific subsets of its training data. in simple words how will the AI act like.  
let's have some examples.  
example 1 : ( career consultant )  
• Non-Technical: "Give me career advice for a data analyst."  
• Technical (Role-Based/identity): Act as a career consultant with 10 years of experience and Give me career advice for a data analyst.  
example 2 : (ghostwriter)  
• Non-Technical: "Write an Instagram summary about AI in 2026 and its impact"  
• Technical (Role-Based/identity):"Act as a ghostwriter with 10 years of experience and write an Instagram summary about AI in 2026 and its impact"
 
**2. Context & Constraints (The "Precision/Boundary" Vector):**  
This is where you define the environment. Without constraints, the AI defaults to "polite filler" text. This is where 90% of prompts fail. You must define the environment and the "walls" the AI cannot climb over. Context provides the "Why" and "Who," while Constraints provide the "No". we will take the same example of the career consultant and ghostwriter here and we will define the context and constraints.  
example 1 : ( career consultant )  
• Context: "The candidate is transitioning from a non-technical role to a Data Analyst role and has a 2-week deadline for their first interview."  
• Constraints: "Do not use clichés like 'think outside the box.' Use Bullet Points only. Limit the response to 3 actionable steps."  
example 2 : (ghostwriter)  
• Context: What is the background? Who is the audience? What is the goal?  
• Constraints: Negative constraints (e.g., "Do not use corporate jargon," "Do not mention [Brand X]") are often more powerful than positive instructions.
 
**3.The "Master Template" (P.R.C.E. Framework):**  
P.R.C.E. stands for Persona, Role, Context, Expectation. It’s a way to structure prompts so the AI knows exactly who to be, what to do, what situation it’s in, and how to deliver the output.  
P (Persona): Who are you? (Expert, Mentor, Critic).It Defines who the AI should act as (e.g.,(for ghostwriter) “brand strategist ghostwriter with 10 years of experience”/(for career consultant)career consultant with 10 years of experience in IT industry).  
R (Result): What exactly do I want? (A blog, a list, a table).- Define what the AI should produce (e.g.,(for ghostwriter) “create a 100‑word Instagram Summary/(for career consultant)list of tools you must know for data analysis”).  
C (Context/Constraints): What is the background? What are the limits? It provides the subject matter or background (e.g., (for ghostwriter)“AI in 2026 and its impact, highlighting our brand’s skills, values, and contributions/(for career consultant)A student who just finished a Python course but has no work experience yet.”).  
E (Execution): Any specific formatting or style? It State the constraints or style requirements.(e.g., (for ghostwriter)“avoid buzzwords, keep tone professional and forward‑looking/(for career consultant)no buzzwords like "passionate" or "hardworking."”).
